Equal Opportunity (EO)


What is "Equal Opportunity"?

Equal Opportunity, also known as EO, is a program designed to ensure fair
and equitable treatment for all soldiers, civilians, and family members
regardless of race, color, gender, religion, national origin,
or sexual orientation. Governed by Army Regulation 600-20 (Chapter 6),
the EO program promotes an environment free from discrimination
and harassment. Each unit has EO Advisors and EO Leaders who assist
commanders by providing guidance, conducting training, and addressing complaints.

Important information

  • Types of EO Complaints

    • Informal: Handled at the lowest level; usually verbal or initial discussion.
    • Formal: Written complaint (DA Form 7279); investigated through the
      chain of command.

  • Key Personnel

    • EO Advisors (EOAs): Trained specialists advising commanders.
    • EO Leaders: Assist in training and addressing EO issues within units.

  • Training

    • All soldiers receive mandatory EO training, including recognition,
      of discrimination, harassment prevention, and complaint procedures.

  • Command Responsibility

    • Commanders are responsible for creating a fair, discrimination-free
      environment and addressing EO complaints promptly.

  • Scope

    • Applies to soldiers, civilian employees, and family members in Army
      programs or activities.
